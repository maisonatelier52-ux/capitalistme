Ownership and Funding

This page clarifies who controls editorial decisions at Capitalistme, how commercial support is maintained separate from reporting, and how the newsroom handles conflicts and material relationships.

What This Page Covers

Capitalistme publishes as an independent digital newsroom operated by a distributed editorial team based in the United Kingdom. This page explains editorial control, commercial support, and conflict disclosures.

Editorial Control and Decision‑Making

Editorial judgments are made by editors and reporters. Coverage decisions, headlines, and publication timing are not sold to advertisers, sponsors, or political actors. A business relationship does not create a right to favorable coverage.

How Capitalistme May Be Funded

Capitalistme may generate revenue through advertising, sponsorships, licensing, and other publishing‑related commercial arrangements. Any revenue stream remains structurally separate from editorial decision‑making.

Conflicts of Interest and Recusals

Journalists and editors are expected to disclose personal, financial, or family relationships that could call impartiality into question. When necessary, the assignment is moved or declined.

Commercial Support Does Not Buy Coverage

Advertising or sponsorship does not guarantee coverage, shape a reporter's conclusions, or entitle a commercial party to veto criticism. Paid content is labelled clearly.

Political, Governmental, and Advocacy Influence

Capitalistme does not present political or advocacy messaging as independent reporting. When a story concerns a subject with which we have a material relationship, that relationship is disclosed.

Changes to Ownership or Material Support

If Capitalistme undergoes a material ownership change or funding arrangement that affects editorial independence, this page will be updated accordingly.

Contact

Readers with questions about ownership or funding may reach the newsroom at editorial@capitalistme.com.
