UK and Switzerland strike landmark £5.2bn services trade deal to boost business growth

By MARCUS ELLINGTON

July 14, 2026

The United Kingdom and Switzerland have agreed a significant trade deal to open up services, digital trade and cross-border commercial prospects. The agreement is anticipated to boost economic relations between the two countries and generate new opportunities for businesses in finance, technology, legal services, consulting and other professional sectors. The deal could enhance UK services exports by roughly £5.2 billion a year in the years ahead, officials believe.

UK Services Industry to Benefit

The deal is meant to facilitate the supply of services across borders and cut red tape, to boost market access for UK enterprises. Easier access to the Swiss market is likely to benefit service providers from fields such as banking, accounting, legal advising, engineering and technology to expand their business and make long-term investments.

Simpler Business Travel and Workforce Mobility

More flexible travel arrangements for professionals are a fundamental part of the deal. Business travelers eligible for the program will be able to work on short-term assignments of up to 90 days a year without a visa. It also facilitates the transfer of skilled individuals by enterprises between the two nations for the purpose of carrying out commercial operations and delivering projects, and eases longer-term transfers of employees.

Digital Trade and the Benefits for Consumers

The deal also includes a range of steps to improve travel comfort and digital connectivity as well as commercial services. It would make cross-border digital trade more seamless, improve protection of intellectual property and bring benefits for travellers, including as faster processing at airports and an end to cell roaming charges when travelling to Switzerland. The adjustments should make company more efficient and improve the consumer experience.

Economic Ties

The new deal builds on the current economic partnership between the UK and Switzerland, boosting collaboration beyond products to high-value service businesses. Businesses from the two countries will benefit from increased certainty, a better investment climate and more prospects for cooperation in industries that contribute meaningfully to economic growth. The pact also embodies a common resolve to keep trade open and modern in an increasingly competitive global economy.

Future prospects

The deal is intended to help boost trade between the two sides, increase investment and provide new chances for growth for businesses operating across the world. Once the legislative and ratification processes are complete, enterprises in both nations will benefit from easier access to markets, increased mobility of workforces and more opportunities in services and digital commerce, further cementing the UK’s position as a leading global services economy.
