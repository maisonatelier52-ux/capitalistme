David Bowie archive to go on UK tour with rare unseen collection

By OLIVER MARSTON

July 1, 2026

Landmark display celebrates David Bowie

Soon, UK music fans and culture lovers will be able to delve into one of the most important collections dedicated to David Bowie. For the first time ever, over 100 carefully selected objects from the iconic artist’s huge archive will be brought together for a future national exhibition, many of which have never been exhibited publicly before. The exhibition is aimed to celebrate Bowie's amazing effect on music, fashion, movies and popular culture and take these significant items to the audiences outside London.

Rare artifacts gives a rare look into Bowie's creativity

Visitors may expect to view a fantastic collection of personal and professional items that trace David Bowie’s creative path. The collection contains legendary stage costumes, handwritten notes, musical instruments, photographs and behind-the-scenes artifacts that show how the artist built his spectacular performances and constantly re-invented his image. It will be a rare opportunity for fans to see some artifacts never before seen in public, offering them a glimpse into his artistic process.

David Bowie archive to be made available to audiences across the UK

The David Bowie collection will visit Dundee to start a UK tour before moving to other cultural locations around the country in the years to come. Organisers hope to take the exhibition on the road so audiences in other parts of the country may experience Bowie's legacy without having to come to the capital. The statewide scheme also represents a rising movement to share key cultural collections with communities across the UK.

Celebrating a legacy in culture that endures

The David Bowie Archive continues to reveal the lasting legacy of one of Britain’s most significant musicians. His work revolutionized music, fashion, and visual narrative, inspiring generations of artists and creators throughout the world. This touring exhibition will allow visitors to obtain insight into Bowie's inventiveness, artistic vision and legacy to Britain culture. The exhibition is set to become one of the UK’s star cultural attractions as it tours the country.
