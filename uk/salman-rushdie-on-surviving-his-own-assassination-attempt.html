Salman Rushdie on surviving his own assassination attempt

By LUCAS HARRINGTON

December 1, 2025

Sir Salman Rushdie, a well-known writer, talked about how he survived an assassination attempt in 2022 while he was speaking at the Chautauqua Institution in New York.The attack shocked the literary world and hurt Rushdie in ways that changed his life.It also got people all over the world talking about free speech.

Rushdie has since talked about the emotional and physical journey that followed in public, thinking about both the pain of the experience and the strength it took to move on.

Rushdie was about to talk about how important it is to be able to think and make art freely when the assassination attempt happened.A man ran up to the platform and attacked him violently, which made people in the audience and security staff step in right away.

He was taken by plane to a hospital and had to have a lot of work done.People all over the world spoke out against the event, and it brought up threats against the author that have been going on for decades.

Rushdie talks about how to get better and move on after the attack

In recent interviews and articles, Rushdie has talked about how hard and long it has been to get better.He has talked openly about the physical, mental, and emotional problems he is having now because of the attack.

Rushdie says that even when things are hard, it is very important for him to keep writing and speaking out for free speech.His thoughts show that he is strong, brave, and ready to tell stories again.

Reactions from all over the world and how they are still important

The event brought up the international debate about how dangerous it is for artists, writers, and public intellectuals.Many people praised Rushdie's strength, saying that his survival was a sign that the fight against censorship and violent extremism was still going on.

Rushdie's thoughts on going back to public life are a strong reminder of how dangerous it can be for writers and how important it is to protect free speech everywhere.
