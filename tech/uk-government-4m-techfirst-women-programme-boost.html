The UK government starts the £4 million TechFirst program to help women in tech

By ISABELLE CROWLEY

March 12, 2026

The UK government has officially taken a big step toward bringing the digital economy back into balance by starting a new subsidy program.Today, the £4 million TechFirst Women's program is being announced.This is a strategic investment meant to break down the long-standing gender barriers in the country's growing digital industry.This program strives to make sure that the UK stays a world leader in innovation by making the most of its varied workforce, which includes both new and experienced experts.

Closing the Gender Gap in STEM

Technology Secretary Liz Kendall came up with the plan as a direct response to the fact that women are still very underrepresented in the technology sector.Research shows that the UK economy loses billions of dollars every year when skilled women leave the workforce, frequently because they don't have enough flexible job options or support systems.The TechFirst Women's program is expressly designed to change this tendency by giving women clear paths to career advancement and admittance into high-growth disciplines like data science and artificial intelligence.

Paid internships and returnship opportunities

This government program is based on 300 paid assignments that provide people hands-on experience in small and medium-sized businesses.These placements are not just short-term jobs; they are set up to provide full career advice and technical training.The program also offers important returnships for experienced workers who have taken breaks from their careers.The government is helping women get back into the workforce with confidence and up-to-date digital skills by giving them a way to get back into top posts.

Motivating the Next Generation of Women Who Create

The TechFirst Girls Competition is not just for existing professionals, but also for the future of the talent pipeline.This nationwide event is for children ages 12 and 13 and encourages young females to learn how to code and solve digital problems.The government wants to get kids interested in STEM subjects for life by teaching them these ideas early on in school.This will make sure that the next generation of UK IT leaders is more diverse and represented than ever before.

Inclusion for a Stronger Economic Future

The £4 million TechFirst Women's program is more than just a social milestone; it's also an important economic strategy.As the need for advanced digital skills grows in fields like banking and healthcare, it is important to expand the pool of people in order to keep growth going.The UK is creating an atmosphere where innovation can thrive by giving women the power to enter, stay, and lead in the tech industry.This will make the tech landscape more resilient and fair in the years to come.
